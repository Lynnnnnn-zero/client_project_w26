Resume Support

Craft a Resume that Tells Your Story

Your resume is often your first impression and an opportunity to highlight your unique strengths, skills, and experiences to potential employers. Whether you are starting from scratch or looking to polish an existing resume, these resources are here to help you every step of the way.

UMSI students are in high demand, and a clear, well-organized resume will help you stand out among applicants. On this page, you will find practical tips, templates, and guides tailored to the needs of School of Information students and aligned with employer expectations.

Resume Writing Made Easy: Steps to Success

  1. Download a UMSI-approved template: Start with a format that works.
  2. Gather your experiences: List jobs, internships, volunteer roles, coursework, and projects most relevant to your goals.
  3. Write impactful bullets: Use action verbs and highlight your skills, results, and contributions.
  4. Tailor for each opportunity: Customize your resume for every job or internship you pursue.
  5. Review and edit: Proofread for errors, check formatting, and ask for feedback.

Resume FAQ

  • How long should my resume be?
    For most UMSI students and new grads, one page is best. More experienced professionals may require two pages.
  • Do I need a different resume for each application?
    It is wise to tailor your resume to the requirements and keywords of each opportunity.
  • Is it okay to include coursework or student projects?
    Absolutely. UMSI coursework and projects are valued by employers, especially if they show relevant skills and impact.
  • How often should I update my resume?
    Update your resume every semester, after major projects, or whenever you gain a new role or responsibility.

Next Steps: Review Checklist

Before you submit your resume, use this quick checklist to make sure it is clear, relevant, and easy for employers to scan.

  • Clarity: Your headings and sections are easy to find.
  • Impact: Bullet points explain what you did and the result.
  • Relevance: The top half of the page matches the role you are applying for.
  • Consistency: Dates, punctuation, and formatting are consistent.
  • Readability: It is easy to scan in 10 seconds.
